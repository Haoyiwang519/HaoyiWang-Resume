Hi, my name is Haoyi Wang or Yugs. I am currently the CEO of SGH Network, as well as the owner and GM of Galaxy News. I am 19 years old, and I am currently a second year at UC Riverside majoring in Pre-Business. The two companies I run are both in the Mobile Esports sector, and they both cover different things. SGH Network is a tournament hosting network, as well as a content network, where we feature the best and most flashy players in the Call of Duty: Mobile and League of Legends: Wild RIft scenes in large scale online events. Galaxy News is a news service that is dedicated to covering important Mobile Esports news across different mobile games as well as regions. We have managed to gain over 18000 members on Discord, as well as nearly a thousand followers on Twitter.

I got into mobile gaming because it was actually my very first opportunity to play games uninterrupted, so I fell in love with the mobile gaming platform. Over the years, I have been everything, from a pro player to a team manager, to an org owner, as well as my current roles. I have over 4 years of experience in Mobile Gaming, and I use that experience to help me make decisions. I am currently based in the Call of Duty: Mobile scene, and I hope to eventually work directly with Activision to produce high quality CODM events for the players that have been grinding for over two years now. While Call of Duty: Mobile is my current focus, I will be shifting focuses in the future in order to cover other games, such as Valorant Mobile, Apex Mobile, Battlefield Mobile, as well as any other upcoming mobile games.

The reason why I chose these companies is because I believed in their vision. For SGH Network, we wanted to provide a place where everyone could compete, regardless of their skill level, and have it be worth their time. I thought this was really important because I remember when I first got into mobile and that there were no oppertunites, and I just happened to get lucky and practiced enough. With Galaxy News, we want to be able to elevate mobile gaming past where it is stuck now, but make it equal and even surpass both console and PC gaming. We can achieve this goal by putting out professional news that are comprable to those in the other scenes, so that even newcomers will be able to understand the comp scene fairly easily.
